Chapter 9: Stigma

Stigma is central to the experience of HIV+ communities. From the start of the pandemic through to now, the stigma associated with the disease has significantly affected our ability to control the transmission of HIV. A history connected to prostitution and drug use, and an association with social taboos such as homosexuality, has ensured that HIV needs to be treated clinically, socially and psychologically. This is a complex mix to get right.
